The Beatles

The band that changed music

The Beatles were an English rock band formed in Liverpool in 1960, comprising John Lennon, Paul McCartney, George Harrison and Ringo Starr.

Here's a timeline of the Beatles:

  • 1956 - At age 16, John Lennon gathers some Quarry Bank Grammar School pals and forms a band called the Quarrymen.
  • 1957 - The Quarrymen perform at St. Peter's Woolton Parish Church. Afterwards, Lennon meets Paul McCartney. McCartney is 15 at the time. Soon after this first meeting, John asks Paul to join the Quarrymen.
  • 1958 - George Harrison joins the Quarrymen. He is 15 years old.
  • 1960 - The Quarr becomes the Silver Beetles and later Silver Beatles.
  • 1961 - Brian Epstein becomes their manager.
  • 1962- "Love Me Do", the band's first single, is released in the United Kingdom. It peaks at #17 on the British Chart.
  • 1963 - The album Please Please Me comes out in the U.K. and is an instant hit there. The album is number one for 29 weeks. "I Want To Hold Your Hand" is number in the U.S and UK.
  • 1964 - The Beatles have 14 songs in Billboard's Hot 100 singles chart.
  • 1965 - The Beatles perform at Shea Stadium in New York for 55,600 people, a record in attendance. The concert grossed $304,000.
  • 1967 - On tour in Bangor Wales the Beatles learn that manager, Brian Epstein has died.
  • 1969 - January - The Beatles perform together for the very last time on the rooftop of Apple Corps Ltd. in London.
  • 1969 - Sept - Although he doesn't announce it publicly due to contract negotiations with the label EMI, Lennon decides to leave the Beatles.
  • 1969 - Sept. 26 - Abbey Road is released in the U.K. where it soars to the top of the charts. It is the Beatles' last studio album.
  • 1970 - McCartney announces that he has left the Beatles.
  • 1980 - Outside his apartment building in New York City, Lennon is shot and killed by Mark David Chapman.
  • 2001 - George Harrison dies of cancer in Los Angeles.
  • 2010 - The entire music catalog of the Beatles is released on itunes.
"They were doing things nobody was doing. Their chords were outrageous, just outrageous, and their harmonies made it all valid… I knew they were pointing the direction of where music had to go.” -- Bob Dylan
